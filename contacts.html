Note

I'm always open to work with competent people who care about quality but...

I receive tons of emails from people who find me via LinkedIn. Most of them haven't even looked at my profile. They just believe they can contact you, ask for your CV without telling a single word about the potential job (as if you were going to accept just anything), pass the CV to someone else (likely other people who just do the same, in a chain of several middlemen) and earn money in return.

That's unfair.

Even when they are the actual employer, some of them think you are going to spend time with them (phone calls, etc.) without any idea about the prospective salary.

And some of them propose me C jobs, probably due to the widespread idea that C and C++ are more or less the same thing, or jobs that contravene my fundamental ethical principles (you won't believe me, but, despite a list like the one that follows being on my LinkedIn profile, at the very start, one person contacted me for a C job for a weapon manufacturer).

So, bear with me if I specify this little list of things that are no-no's for me, if only for the few individuals who will actually read it.

Please, don't contact me:

  • For military applications.
  • If the job you propose involves “consulting” companies or any middleman which steals money without adding value (it's OK if they are just recruiters and disappear once the work relationship is established; it's not OK if they keep subtracting money after that).
  • For work in C.
  • If you don't mention the salary range.
  • If you can't show examples of the code you produce, before the first interview (I can show examples of mine).
  • If you plan for me to train other people (I don't like teaching).
